RNN:
The model manages to generate some convincing names such as "shobhit" , "shivangi" , "venkat" etc.

But still some names are not good and unrealistic e.g. "kamom" , "hishi" , "teim"
Some malformed endings and rare-letter noise exist.

Overall: best balance of realism and diversity.




BLSTM:

This model shows very high novelity / diversity but it is the most terrible model when generating names.
Names are not realistic at all.

Also there are some artifacts
Overall: diverse but weaker realism.



RNN + Attention:

Only manages to generate few names realistically e.g. shara , shiva , shana  , sita.
Strong repetition of a few patterns (for example repeated “sur”/“sura”  and their variants).
Overall: lower qualitative diversity despite acceptable diversity.
